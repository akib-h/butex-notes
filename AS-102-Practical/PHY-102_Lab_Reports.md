## Experiment 01 — Vernier Calipers: Width of a Rectangular Object

**Apparatus:** Vernier calipers, rectangular object

**Formula:**
$$\text{Least Count (LC)} = \frac{\text{1 MSD}}{n} = \frac{x}{n}$$
$$\text{Correct Reading} = \text{Observed Reading} - \text{Zero Error (with sign)}$$

**Observation:**
- Total divisions on Vernier scale, $n = 20$
- Value of 1 MSD, $x = 0.1\ \text{cm} = 1\ \text{mm}$
- **LC** $= \dfrac{1}{20} = 0.05\ \text{mm}$

**Zero Error Table** (Positive, +ve assumed):

| SL | MSR (mm) | Coinciding VSD | VS Reading = VSD × LC (mm) | Total (mm) |
|----|----------|---------------|---------------------------|------------|
| 1  | 0        | 8             | 8 × 0.05 = 0.40           | 0.40       |
| 2  | 0        | 7             | 7 × 0.05 = 0.35           | 0.35       |
| 3  | 0        | 8             | 8 × 0.05 = 0.40           | 0.40       |
| 4  | 0        | 9             | 9 × 0.05 = 0.45           | 0.45       |

**Mean Zero Error** $= \dfrac{0.40+0.35+0.40+0.45}{4} = 0.40\ \text{mm}$

**Width Table:**

| SL | MSR (mm) | Coinciding VSD | VS Reading (mm) | Total (mm) |
|----|----------|---------------|-----------------|------------|
| 1  | 19       | 8             | 0.40            | 19.40      |
| 2  | 19       | 7             | 0.35            | 19.35      |
| 3  | 19       | 8             | 0.40            | 19.40      |
| 4  | 18.9     | 9             | 0.45            | 19.35      |
| 5  | 19.1     | 8             | 0.40            | 19.50      |

**Mean Observed Width** $= \dfrac{19.40+19.35+19.40+19.35+19.50}{5} = \dfrac{97.00}{5} = 19.40\ \text{mm}$

**Correct Width** $= 19.40 - 0.40 = \boxed{19.00\ \text{mm}}$

> **Result:** Width of the rectangular object = **19.00 mm**

## Experiment 03 — Focal Length of a Convex Lens (Displacement Method)

**Apparatus:** Optical bench, convex lens, image screen, light source

**Theory:** When object–screen distance $D > 4f$, two lens positions $L_1$, $L_2$ give sharp images. With displacement $x = L_1 \sim L_2$:

$$\boxed{f = \frac{D^2 - x^2}{4D}} \qquad P = \frac{100}{f'\ (\text{cm})}\ \text{D}$$

**Data Table:**

| $n$ | $O$ (cm) | $I$ (cm) | $L_1$ (cm) | $L_2$ (cm) | $x = L_1 \sim L_2$ (cm) | $D = I - O$ (cm) | $f$ (cm) |
|-----|----------|----------|-----------|-----------|------------------------|-----------------|---------|
| 1   | 0        | 89       | 36.6      | 54.0      | 17.4                   | 89              | 21.40   |
| 2   | 0        | 90       | 36.0      | 53.5      | 17.5                   | 90              | 21.65   |
| 3   | 0        | 92       | 37.0      | 56.1      | 19.1                   | 92              | 22.01   |
| 4   | 0        | 94       | 36.3      | 58.8      | 22.5                   | 94              | 22.15   |
| 5   | 0        | 98       | 33.6      | 64.0      | 30.4                   | 98              | 22.14   |

**Sample Calculation ($n = 1$):**
$$f_1 = \frac{89^2 - 17.4^2}{4 \times 89} = \frac{7921 - 302.76}{356} = \frac{7618.24}{356} = 21.40\ \text{cm}$$

**Mean Focal Length:**
$$f' = \frac{21.40+21.65+22.01+22.15+22.14}{5} = 21.87\ \text{cm}$$

**Power:**
$$P = \frac{100}{21.87} = 4.57\ \text{D}$$

**Percentage Error:**
$$\%\ \text{error} = \left|\frac{21.87 - 20}{20}\right| \times 100 = 9.35\%$$

> **Result:** Focal length $f' = \textbf{21.87 cm}$, Power $P = \textbf{4.57 D}$

## Experiment 04 — Acceleration Due to Gravity (Simple Pendulum)

**Apparatus:** Bob, thread, stop clock, metre scale, Vernier calipers, clamp stand

**Theory:**
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{l}{g}} \implies \boxed{g = 4\pi^2 \cdot \frac{l}{T^2}}$$

**Radius of Bob:**

| SL | MSR (cm) | Coinciding VSD | Diameter (cm) |
|----|----------|---------------|---------------|
| 1  | 1.11     | 6             | 1.140         |
| 2  | 1.12     | 4             | 1.140         |
| 3  | 1.11     | 7             | 1.145         |

Mean diameter $= 1.142\ \text{cm}$ → Radius $r = 0.57\ \text{cm}$, $l_1\ (\text{hook}) = 0.21\ \text{cm}$

**Time Period Table** (10 vibrations each, $l = l_\text{thread} + l_\text{hook} + r$):

| SL | $l_2$ (cm) | Eff. $l$ (cm) | $t_1$ (s) | $t_2$ (s) | $t_3$ (s) | Mean $t$ (s) | $T = t/10$ (s) | $l/T^2$ |
|----|-----------|--------------|---------|---------|---------|------------|--------------|---------|
| 1  | 34        | 34.78        | 11.94   | 12.00   | 11.92   | 11.95      | 1.195        | 24.36   |
| 2  | 42        | 42.78        | 13.28   | 13.25   | 13.27   | 13.26      | 1.326        | 24.33   |

**Mean** $l/T^2 = \dfrac{24.36+24.33+23.31}{3} = 23.999\ \text{cm/s}^2$

## Experiment 06 — Moment of Inertia of a Flywheel

**Apparatus:** Flywheel, stop watch, set square, weight, metre scale, Vernier calipers

**Theory:**
$$mgh = \tfrac{1}{2}M_0\omega^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}I\omega^2 + n_1 f \tag{i}$$
$$\tfrac{1}{2}I\omega^2 = n_2 f \tag{ii}$$

Average angular velocity after detachment: $\omega_a = \dfrac{\omega}{2} = \dfrac{2\pi n_2}{t}$, so $\omega = \dfrac{4\pi n_2}{t}$

$$\boxed{I = \frac{2Mgh - M\omega^2 r^2}{\omega^2\!\left(1 + \dfrac{n_1}{n_2}\right)}}$$

**Radius of Axle** (LC = 0.005 cm):

| SL | MSR (cm) | VSD | Diameter (cm) | Radius (cm) |
|----|----------|-----|---------------|-------------|
| a1 | 2.10     | 4   | 2.120         | 1.060       |
| a2 | 2.05     | 3   | 2.065         | 1.033       |
| a3 | 2.07     | 4   | 2.120         | 1.060       |
| b1 | 2.07     | 5   | 2.095         | 1.048       |
| b2 | 2.10     | 5   | 2.095         | 1.048       |
| b3 | 2.04     | 4   | 2.120         | 1.060       |

**Mean radius** $r = 1.05\ \text{cm}$

**$n_1$, $n_2$, $t$ Table:**

| Obs | $M$ (gm) | $h$ (cm) | $n_1$ | Mean $n_1$ | $n_2$ | Mean $n_2$ | $t$ (s) | Mean $t$ (s) |
|-----|---------|---------|------|-----------|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| 01  | 500     | 100     | 10   |           | 11   |           | 7.0    |            |
| 02  | 500     | 150     | 11   | 11        | 17   | 14.67     | 8.5    | 7.97       |
| 03  | 500     | 100     | 12   |           | 16   |           | 8.4    |            |
| 01  | 1500    | 150     | 11   |           | 26   |           | —      |            |
| 02  | 1500    | 100     | 10   | 11        | 29   | 26.67     | —      | 11.24      |
| 03  | 1500    | 100     | 12   |           | 25   |           | —      |            |

**Calculation:**

**For $M = 500\ \text{gm}$**, $h = 150\ \text{cm}$, $n_1=11$, $n_2=14.67$, $t=7.97\ \text{s}$:

$$\omega_1 = \frac{4\pi \times 14.67}{7.97} = \frac{184.35}{7.97} = 23.13\ \text{rad/s}$$

$$I_1 = \frac{2(500)(980)(150) - 500(23.13)^2(1.05)^2}{(23.13)^2\!\left(1+\frac{11}{14.67}\right)} = \frac{147{,}000{,}000 - 294{,}919}{535.0 \times 1.750} = \frac{146{,}705{,}081}{936.25} = 156{,}693\ \text{gm·cm}^2$$

**For $M = 1500\ \text{gm}$**, $h = 150\ \text{cm}$, $n_1=11$, $n_2=26.67$, $t=11.24\ \text{s}$:

$$\omega_2 = \frac{4\pi \times 26.67}{11.24} = \frac{335.19}{11.24} = 29.82\ \text{rad/s}$$

$$I_2 = \frac{2(1500)(980)(150) - 1500(29.82)^2(1.05)^2}{(29.82)^2\!\left(1+\frac{11}{26.67}\right)} = \frac{441{,}000{,}000 - 1{,}470{,}720}{889.2 \times 1.4124} = \frac{439{,}529{,}280}{1{,}255.9} = 349{,}932\ \text{gm·cm}^2$$

$$\boxed{I = \frac{I_1+I_2}{2} = \frac{156{,}693+349{,}932}{2} = 253{,}313\ \text{gm·cm}^2}$$

> **Result:** Moment of Inertia of the flywheel $I = \textbf{253,313 gm·cm}^2 \approx \textbf{25.33 \times 10}^{-3}\ \textbf{kg·m}^2$

## 📚 Additional References & Extended Knowledge

> *These are supplementary — separate from the main exam notes above.*

### Vernier Calipers
- **Standard Least Count Values:** 0.1 mm (10 VSD), 0.05 mm (20 VSD), 0.02 mm (50 VSD)
- **Ref:** Serway & Jewett, *Physics for Scientists and Engineers*, 9th ed., Ch. 1
- NIST Guide to SI units: [physics.nist.gov](https://physics.nist.gov)

### Screw Gauge (Micrometer)
- Typical pitch = 0.5 mm (not 1 mm) for metric micrometers; LC = 0.5/50 = **0.01 mm**
- Backlash error: always rotate screw in **one direction only**
- **Ref:** H.C. Verma, *Concepts of Physics*, Vol. 1, Ch. 2

### Convex Lens — Focal Length
- Displacement method first described by **Bessel (1840)** — also called *Bessel's method*
- Condition: $D > 4f$ strictly; optimal accuracy when $D \approx 1.5–2 \times 4f$
- Standard value: typical lab lens $f \approx 20\ \text{cm}$ (matches 20 cm used for % error above)
- **Ref:** Hecht, *Optics*, 5th ed., §5.2; Jenkins & White, *Fundamentals of Optics*, Ch. 3

### Simple & Compound Pendulum
| Location | Accepted $g$ (m/s²) |
|----------|-------------------|
| Equator  | 9.780              |
| Bangladesh (~23°N) | 9.786      |
| Poles    | 9.832              |
| Standard | **9.806**          |

- Simple pendulum valid only for $\theta < 5°$
- Compound pendulum more accurate due to elimination of string mass error
- **Ref:** Kater (1818), *Phil. Trans. Royal Society*; BIPM standard value of $g$

### Moment of Inertia — Flywheel
- Theoretical $I$ for a solid disk: $I = \tfrac{1}{2}MR^2$
- Theoretical $I$ for a ring: $I = \tfrac{1}{2}M(R_1^2 + R_2^2)$
- Bearing friction $f$ is often non-negligible; the ratio $n_1/n_2$ accounts for it
- **Ref:** Irodov, *Problems in General Physics*, Problem 1.283; Marion & Thornton, *Classical Dynamics*, Ch. 9

### Key Formula Sheet (Quick Ref)
$$f = \frac{D^2-x^2}{4D} \qquad g = \frac{4\pi^2 l}{T^2} \qquad I = \frac{2Mgh - M\omega^2r^2}{\omega^2(1+n_1/n_2)} \qquad \omega = \frac{4\pi n_2}{t}$$
$$\text{LC}_\text{vernier} = \frac{x}{n} \qquad \text{LC}_\text{screw} = \frac{\text{pitch}}{N} \qquad P\ (\text{D}) = \frac{100}{f\ (\text{cm})}$$
