## Question 1: Ionic Radii of Fe, Fe¬≤‚Āļ, and Fe¬≥‚Āļ

**Q: Compare and explain the radii of Fe, Fe¬≤‚Āļ, and Fe¬≥‚Āļ (5 marks)**

### Answer:

**Radius Order:** Fe (126 pm) > Fe¬≤‚Āļ (78 pm) > Fe¬≥‚Āļ (64.5 pm)

**Electronic Configurations:**
- Fe: [Ar] 3d‚Ā∂ 4s¬≤ (26 electrons)
- Fe¬≤‚Āļ: [Ar] 3d‚Ā∂ (24 electrons)
- Fe¬≥‚Āļ: [Ar] 3d‚ĀĶ (23 electrons)

**Explanation:**

1. **Fe ‚Üí Fe¬≤‚Āļ:** Loss of two 4s electrons reduces radius significantly
   - Outer boundary moves from 4s to 3d orbital (closer to nucleus)
   - Fewer electrons ‚Üí increased effective nuclear charge (Zeff)
   - Same 26 protons pulling on fewer electrons

2. **Fe¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Fe¬≥‚Āļ:** Further removal of one 3d electron
   - Even higher Zeff (26 protons on only 23 electrons)
   - Reduced electron-electron repulsion
   - Electrons pulled closer to nucleus

3. **Key Principle:** r ‚ąĚ 1/Zeff
   - As electrons are removed, Zeff increases
   - Radius decreases proportionally

**Conclusion:** Cations are always smaller than their parent atoms due to loss of electrons and increased nuclear attraction per electron.

## Question 2: Ionic Radii of O, O‚ĀĽ, and O¬≤‚ĀĽ

**Q: Compare and explain the radii of O, O‚ĀĽ, and O¬≤‚ĀĽ (5 marks)**

### Answer:

**Radius Order:** O¬≤‚ĀĽ (140 pm) > O‚ĀĽ (176 pm) > O (60 pm)

**Electronic Configurations:**
- O: 1s¬≤ 2s¬≤ 2p‚Āī (8 electrons, 8 protons)
- O‚ĀĽ: 1s¬≤ 2s¬≤ 2p‚ĀĶ (9 electrons, 8 protons)
- O¬≤‚ĀĽ: 1s¬≤ 2s¬≤ 2p‚Ā∂ (10 electrons, 8 protons)

**Explanation:**

1. **O ‚Üí O‚ĀĽ:** Addition of one electron increases radius
   - Extra electron increases electron-electron repulsion
   - Same nuclear charge (8+) spread over more electrons
   - Decreased Zeff per electron ‚Üí expansion

2. **O‚ĀĽ ‚Üí O¬≤‚ĀĽ:** Addition of second electron further increases radius
   - Now complete octet configuration like Ne
   - Maximum repulsion with 10 electrons vs 8 protons
   - Significantly expanded electron cloud
   - O¬≤‚ĀĽ is much larger due to low Zeff

3. **Note:** O¬≤‚ĀĽ radius (140 pm) >> O radius (60 pm) - more than double!
   - Anions are ALWAYS larger than parent atoms
   - Multiple negative charges cause dramatic expansion

**Key Point:** Adding electrons increases repulsion and decreases effective nuclear charge, causing radius expansion.

## Question 3: Isoelectronic Series Comparison

**Q: Explain the trend in radii for the isoelectronic series: O¬≤‚ĀĽ, F‚ĀĽ, Ne, Na‚Āļ, Mg¬≤‚Āļ, Al¬≥‚Āļ (5 marks)**

### Answer:

**All species have 10 electrons [1s¬≤ 2s¬≤ 2p‚Ā∂] - Neon configuration**

**Radius Order:** O¬≤‚ĀĽ (140 pm) > F‚ĀĽ (133 pm) > Ne (112 pm) > Na‚Āļ (102 pm) > Mg¬≤‚Āļ (72 pm) > Al¬≥‚Āļ (53.5 pm)

| Species | Protons | Electrons | Nuclear Charge | Radius (pm) |
|---------|---------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| O¬≤‚ĀĽ | 8 | 10 | Lowest (+8) | 140 (largest) |
| F‚ĀĽ | 9 | 10 | Low (+9) | 133 |
| Ne | 10 | 10 | Neutral (+10) | 112 |
| Na‚Āļ | 11 | 10 | High (+11) | 102 |
| Mg¬≤‚Āļ | 12 | 10 | Higher (+12) | 72 |
| Al¬≥‚Āļ | 13 | 10 | Highest (+13) | 53.5 (smallest) |

**Explanation:**

1. **Same number of electrons** (10) but **different nuclear charges**
2. **Higher nuclear charge ‚Üí stronger attraction ‚Üí smaller radius**
3. **Zeff increases** across the series: O¬≤‚ĀĽ < F‚ĀĽ < Ne < Na‚Āļ < Mg¬≤‚Āļ < Al¬≥‚Āļ
4. **Al¬≥‚Āļ is smallest:** 13 protons pulling on 10 electrons very strongly
5. **O¬≤‚ĀĽ is largest:** Only 8 protons for 10 electrons, weak attraction

**Formula:** For isoelectronic species, r ‚ąĚ 1/Z (inversely proportional to nuclear charge)

## Question 4: Periodic Trends in Atomic Radii

**Q: Explain the periodic trends in atomic radii across a period and down a group (5 marks)**

### Answer:

### **Trend 1: Across a Period (Left ‚Üí Right)**

**Radius DECREASES**

**Example - Period 3:**
- Na (186 pm) > Mg (160 pm) > Al (143 pm) > Si (117 pm) > P (110 pm) > S (104 pm) > Cl (99 pm) > Ar (97 pm)

**Reasons:**
1. **Increasing nuclear charge** (Na: 11+ ‚Üí Cl: 17+)
2. **Same shielding** (electrons added to same shell - 3s/3p)
3. **Increasing Zeff** pulls electrons closer
4. **Same principal quantum number** (n=3)

**Key Equation:** Zeff = Z - S (shielding constant S remains ~constant)

---

### **Trend 2: Down a Group (Top ‚Üí Bottom)**

**Radius INCREASES**

**Example - Group 1 (Alkali Metals):**
- Li (152 pm) < Na (186 pm) < K (227 pm) < Rb (248 pm) < Cs (265 pm)

**Reasons:**
1. **Additional electron shells** (Li: n=2, Na: n=3, K: n=4, etc.)
2. **Increased shielding** from inner electrons
3. **Decreased Zeff** felt by outer electrons
4. **Distance from nucleus increases** with each new shell

**Key Principle:** The addition of a new shell outweighs increased nuclear charge

---

### **Summary:**

```
Atomic Radius Trends:

        DECREASES ‚Üí
    Li   Be   B    C    N    O    F
    ‚Üď
    Na   Mg   Al   Si   P    S    Cl
    ‚Üď
    K    Ca   Ga   Ge   As   Se   Br
    
INCREASES ‚Üď
```

## Question 5: Cation vs Anion Size

**Q: Why are cations smaller and anions larger than their parent atoms? Give examples (5 marks)**

### Answer:

### **Cations (Positive Ions) - SMALLER than parent atoms**

**Examples:**

| Atom | Radius (pm) | Cation | Radius (pm) | Change |
|------|-------------|--------|-------------|--------|
| Na | 186 | Na‚Āļ | 102 | -45% |
| Mg | 160 | Mg¬≤‚Āļ | 72 | -55% |
| Al | 143 | Al¬≥‚Āļ | 53.5 | -63% |
| Fe | 126 | Fe¬≥‚Āļ | 64.5 | -49% |

**Reasons:**

1. **Loss of valence shell:** Often entire outer shell is removed
   - Na loses 3s¬Ļ ‚Üí outer shell becomes 2p‚Ā∂
   
2. **Increased Zeff:** Same protons, fewer electrons
   - Na: 11p‚Āļ on 11e‚ĀĽ ‚Üí Na‚Āļ: 11p‚Āļ on 10e‚ĀĽ
   
3. **Reduced repulsion:** Fewer electrons mean less mutual repulsion

4. **Greater nuclear pull:** Each remaining electron feels stronger attraction

---

### **Anions (Negative Ions) - LARGER than parent atoms**

**Examples:**

| Atom | Radius (pm) | Anion | Radius (pm) | Change |
|------|-------------|-------|-------------|--------|
| F | 64 | F‚ĀĽ | 133 | +108% |
| O | 60 | O¬≤‚ĀĽ | 140 | +133% |
| Cl | 99 | Cl‚ĀĽ | 181 | +83% |
| S | 104 | S¬≤‚ĀĽ | 184 | +77% |

**Reasons:**

1. **Added electrons:** Extra electrons in same or outer shell

2. **Decreased Zeff:** Same protons, more electrons
   - F: 9p‚Āļ on 9e‚ĀĽ ‚Üí F‚ĀĽ: 9p‚Āļ on 10e‚ĀĽ

3. **Increased repulsion:** More electrons push each other apart

4. **Weaker nuclear control:** Nuclear charge cannot hold extra electrons as tightly

---

### **General Rules:**

```
Cation formation: Atom - e‚ĀĽ ‚Üí Smaller radius
Anion formation: Atom + e‚ĀĽ ‚Üí Larger radius

Magnitude: Anion expansion > Cation contraction (relatively)
```

## Question 6: Ionic Radii and Bonding

**Q: How does ionic radius affect bond strength and lattice energy? (5 marks)**

### Answer:

### **1. Relationship Between Ionic Radius and Lattice Energy**

**Lattice Energy (U):** Energy required to separate one mole of ionic solid into gaseous ions

**Born-Land√© Equation (simplified):**
```
U ‚ąĚ (Z‚Āļ √ó Z‚ĀĽ) / (r‚Āļ + r‚ĀĽ)

Where:
Z‚Āļ, Z‚ĀĽ = charges on ions
r‚Āļ, r‚ĀĽ = ionic radii
```

**Key Principle:** Smaller ions ‚Üí Higher lattice energy

---

### **2. Examples:**

| Compound | Cation Radius | Anion Radius | Lattice Energy (kJ/mol) |
|----------|---------------|--------------|-------------------------|
| LiF | Li‚Āļ: 76 pm | F‚ĀĽ: 133 pm | 1037 (highest) |
| NaF | Na‚Āļ: 102 pm | F‚ĀĽ: 133 pm | 923 |
| KF | K‚Āļ: 138 pm | F‚ĀĽ: 133 pm | 821 |
| LiCl | Li‚Āļ: 76 pm | Cl‚ĀĽ: 181 pm | 853 |
| LiBr | Li‚Āļ: 76 pm | Br‚ĀĽ: 196 pm | 807 (lowest) |

**Observation:** 
- **Smaller ions** (Li‚Āļ, F‚ĀĽ) ‚Üí **Stronger ionic bonds** ‚Üí Higher lattice energy
- **Larger ions** (K‚Āļ, Br‚ĀĽ) ‚Üí **Weaker ionic bonds** ‚Üí Lower lattice energy

---

### **3. Effect on Physical Properties**

**Melting Point:**
- Smaller ions ‚Üí Higher lattice energy ‚Üí Higher melting point
- MgO (Mg¬≤‚Āļ: 72 pm, O¬≤‚ĀĽ: 140 pm): MP = 2852¬įC
- CaO (Ca¬≤‚Āļ: 100 pm, O¬≤‚ĀĽ: 140 pm): MP = 2613¬įC
- BaO (Ba¬≤‚Āļ: 135 pm, O¬≤‚Āļ: 140 pm): MP = 1923¬įC

**Solubility:**
- Depends on balance between lattice energy and hydration energy
- Small, highly charged ions: High lattice energy, may be less soluble

---

### **4. Polarization and Covalent Character**

**Fajans' Rules:**
- **Small cation** + **Large anion** ‚Üí More covalent character
- Example: LiI has more covalent character than NaI
  - Li‚Āļ (76 pm) is smaller, more polarizing
  - I‚ĀĽ (220 pm) is large, more polarizable

**Polarizing Power:** Charge/radius ratio
- Fe¬≥‚Āļ/r = 3/64.5 = 0.0465 (high polarizing power)
- Na‚Āļ/r = 1/102 = 0.0098 (low polarizing power)

## Question 8: Lanthanide Contraction

**Q: Explain lanthanide contraction and its consequences (5 marks)**

### Answer:

### **Definition:**

**Lanthanide Contraction:** The steady decrease in atomic and ionic radii of lanthanide elements (Ce to Lu) despite adding electrons to the 4f orbital.

---

### **Radii Data (Ln¬≥‚Āļ ions):**

| Ion | Atomic No. | Radius (pm) |
|-----|------------|-------------|
| La¬≥‚Āļ | 57 | 103.2 |
| Ce¬≥‚Āļ | 58 | 101.0 |
| Pr¬≥‚Āļ | 59 | 99.0 |
| Nd¬≥‚Āļ | 60 | 98.3 |
| Sm¬≥‚Āļ | 62 | 95.8 |
| Eu¬≥‚Āļ | 63 | 94.7 |
| Gd¬≥‚Āļ | 64 | 93.8 |
| Dy¬≥‚Āļ | 66 | 91.2 |
| Er¬≥‚Āļ | 68 | 89.0 |
| Yb¬≥‚Āļ | 70 | 86.8 |
| Lu¬≥‚Āļ | 71 | 86.1 |

**Total decrease:** ~17 pm across the series

---

### **Cause:**

1. **Poor shielding by 4f electrons**
   - f-orbitals have poor shielding ability
   - Even worse than d-orbital shielding
   
2. **Increasing nuclear charge**
   - Each element adds one proton
   - La: 57+ ‚Üí Lu: 71+
   
3. **Incomplete shielding**
   - Added 4f electrons don't compensate for increased nuclear charge
   - Zeff increases steadily
   
4. **Contraction of 5s and 5p orbitals**
   - Outer orbitals pulled inward by increasing Zeff

---

### **Consequences:**

**1. Similar Radii of 4d and 5d Elements**
- Zr‚Āī‚Āļ (72 pm) ‚Čą Hf‚Āī‚Āļ (71 pm)
- Nb‚ĀĶ‚Āļ (64 pm) ‚Čą Ta‚ĀĶ‚Āļ (64 pm)
- Makes separation very difficult

**2. Similar Chemistry**
- Zr and Hf almost identical chemically
- Found together in nature
- Hard to separate industrially

**3. Increased Density of 5d Elements**
- Smaller size with similar mass ‚Üí higher density
- W (tungsten): density = 19.3 g/cm¬≥
- Mo (molybdenum): density = 10.3 g/cm¬≥

**4. Basic Character Decreases**
- Smaller Ln¬≥‚Āļ ions are less basic
- La(OH)‚āÉ > Lu(OH)‚āÉ in basicity

**5. Coordination Number Changes**
- Early lanthanides: CN = 9-12
- Late lanthanides: CN = 6-8

---

### **Comparison: d-Block vs f-Block Contraction**

| Property | d-Block | f-Block |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Magnitude** | Small (~15 pm) | Larger (~17 pm) |
| **Regularity** | Irregular | More regular |
| **Cause** | Poor d-shielding | Very poor f-shielding |
| **Effect** | Moderate | Pronounced |
