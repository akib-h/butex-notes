### **Question 3: Electrochemistry**

**Q:** Calculate the equilibrium constant for the disproportionation reaction:

### **Question 4: Complex Formation**

**Q:** Explain why [Fe(Hв‚‚O)в‚†]ВІвЃє is pale green while [Fe(Hв‚‚O)в‚†]ВівЃє is pale yellow.

**Solution:**

- Electronic configuration: 3dвЃ¶
- In octahedral field: tв‚‚gвЃґ egВІ

- Both complexes are high-spin due to weak field Hв‚‚O ligands
- The different d-electron counts lead to different absorption spectra
- FeВівЃє complexes generally have higher О”в‚’ values than FeВІвЃє due to higher charge

### **Question 5: Biological Significance**

**Q:** Explain the role of FeВІвЃє and FeВівЃє in biological systems, particularly in hemoglobin and iron storage.

**Solution:**

**Hemoglobin and Oxygen Transport (FeВІвЃє):**
- Hemoglobin contains iron in the **FeВІвЃє state** coordinated to a porphyrin ring (heme group)

**Iron Storage and Transport:**
- **Ferritin**: Stores iron primarily as **FeВівЃє** (more stable, less toxic)
- **Transferrin**: Transports FeВівЃє in blood plasma
- **Ferrous form (FeВІвЃє)**: Better absorbed in the intestine

**Redox Cycling:**
```
Intestinal absorption: FeВівЃє (diet) в†’ FeВІвЃє (absorbed)
```

**Importance:**
- FeВІвЃє/FeВівЃє balance is crucial for oxygen transport, electron transfer in cellular respiration, and enzyme function
- Disruption leads to anemia or iron overload disorders
