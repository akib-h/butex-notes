## 📖 Topics Covered

### 1. [Complex Compounds (Coordination Chemistry)](./complex-compounds/)

Comprehensive coverage of coordination chemistry including:

- **Introduction & Historical Context**
  - Alfred Werner's pioneering work
  - Development of coordination chemistry
  - First coordination compound (Prussian Blue)

- **Werner's Theory**
  - Primary and secondary valencies
  - Coordination number concepts
  - Geometrical arrangements

- **Ligands Classification**
  - Monodentate, bidentate, and polydentate ligands
  - Chelate effect and stability
  - Ambidentate and flexidentate ligands
  - π-donor and π-acceptor ligands

- **Nomenclature**
  - IUPAC naming rules
  - Complex ion notation
  - Practice examples

- **Isomerism**
  - Structural isomerism (linkage, coordination, ionization, hydrate)
  - Stereoisomerism (geometrical and optical)
  - Medical applications (cis-platin)

- **Bonding Theories**
  - Valence Bond Theory (VBT)
  - Crystal Field Theory (CFT)
  - Ligand Field Theory (LFT)
  - Angular Overlap Model (AOM)

- **Crystal Field Splitting**
  - Octahedral, tetrahedral, square planar complexes
  - High-spin vs low-spin configurations
  - Spectrochemical series
  - Jahn-Teller distortion

- **Applications**
  - Biological systems (hemoglobin, chlorophyll)
  - Industrial catalysis
  - Medical applications
  - Analytical chemistry

### 2. [Acids and Bases](./acids-and-bases/)

In-depth exploration of acid-base chemistry:

- **Arrhenius Theory**
  - Historical context and definitions
  - Limitations and scope
  - Neutralization reactions

- **Brønsted-Lowry Theory**
  - Proton donor-acceptor concept
  - Conjugate acid-base pairs
  - Amphoteric substances
  - Non-aqueous reactions

- **Lewis Theory**
  - Electron pair donor-acceptor concept
  - Relationship to coordination chemistry
  - Hard-Soft Acid-Base (HSAB) theory
  - Broader applications

- **Acid-Base Strength**
  - Strong vs weak acids and bases
  - Dissociation constants (Ka, Kb)
  - pKa and pKb values
  - Conjugate pair relationships

- **pH Scale**
  - Definition and calculations
  - pH, pOH, and pKw relationships
  - pH of strong and weak acids/bases
  - Practical pH measurements

- **Buffer Solutions**
  - Composition and mechanism
  - Buffer capacity and range
  - Biological buffer systems
  - Blood pH regulation

- **Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation**
  - Derivation and applications
  - Buffer pH calculations
  - Limitations and valid ranges
  - Worked examples

- **Acid-Base Titrations**
  - Types of titrations
  - Titration curves
  - Equivalence point determination
  - Indicator selection

### 3. [Analytical Analysis (Gravimetric Methods)](./analytical-analysis/)

Detailed coverage of gravimetric analytical techniques:

- **Introduction to Gravimetric Analysis**
  - Principles and definitions
  - Types of gravimetric methods
  - Advantages and limitations
  - Historical significance

- **Precipitation Gravimetry**
  - Ideal precipitate properties
  - Precipitation mechanism (nucleation and growth)
  - Relative supersaturation (RSS)
  - Particle size control
  - Digestion process
  - Coprecipitation and post-precipitation
  - Common precipitating agents
  - Detailed procedures (Ni, BaSO₄ determinations)

- **Volatilization Gravimetry**
  - Direct and indirect methods
  - Water (moisture) content determination
  - Carbon dioxide from carbonates
  - Organic compound analysis
  - Apparatus and techniques

- **Electrogravimetry**
  - Electrochemical deposition
  - Controlled potential methods
  - Metal analysis applications
  - Advantages and selectivity

- **Particulate Gravimetry**
  - Total Suspended Solids (TSS)
  - Air quality monitoring
  - Environmental applications
  - Filter selection and procedures

- **Experimental Procedures**
  - Step-by-step protocols
  - Laboratory techniques
  - Equipment usage
  - Safety considerations

- **Calculations and Data Analysis**
  - Gravimetric factors
  - Stoichiometric calculations
  - Statistical analysis
  - Error analysis

- **Sources of Error**
  - Systematic errors
  - Random errors
  - Error minimization strategies
