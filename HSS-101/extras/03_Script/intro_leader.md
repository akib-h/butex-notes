### 📋 PREPARED Q&A RESPONSES

> Every member should be comfortable with all answers. The Leader triages questions — pointing to the best-suited member for each.

---

**Q: What is the difference between AI and Machine Learning?**

*Suggested speaker: Member 3 or Leader*

**"AI is the broad concept — any machine that mimics intelligent behavior. Machine Learning is a specific approach to building AI, where the system learns from data rather than following hard-coded rules. So all machine learning is AI, but not all AI is machine learning. Think of it as: AI is the destination, machine learning is one of the main roads to get there."**

---

**Q: Is AGI possible in our lifetime?**

*Suggested speaker: Member 6 or Leader*

**"Opinions vary widely — and that's not a politician's non-answer, it's a genuine reflection of the field. Some leading researchers, including Geoffrey Hinton, believe it could happen within decades. Others argue the gap between narrow task performance and true general reasoning is much wider than it appears. What we can say is: the field is advancing faster than most predictions anticipated — and that uncertainty is precisely why AI safety research matters."**

---

**Q: Will AI take my job?**

*Suggested speaker: Member 6*

**"Some tasks — especially repetitive, data-heavy ones — are at high risk of automation. But AI also creates new jobs and augments existing ones. The key is adaptability. Learning to work alongside AI tools, rather than treating them as competitors, is the most practical strategy for anyone entering the workforce today. The Industrial Revolution looked terrifying from the inside too — and it eventually produced more jobs than it destroyed. The honest answer: we don't know yet. Which is why the governance and transition planning matters so much."**

---

**Q: Is ChatGPT actually intelligent?**

*Suggested speaker: Member 3*

**"ChatGPT is what we call Narrow AI — extraordinarily good at one thing: generating contextually appropriate text based on patterns learned from enormous amounts of human-written data. It doesn't understand in the way humans do. It doesn't have beliefs, feelings, or intentions. It can produce confident-sounding errors — which is actually a safety concern. It's a very sophisticated prediction machine — impressive beyond anything previously built, but not human intelligence."**

---

**Q: How is AI regulated in Bangladesh?**

*Suggested speaker: Member 4*

**"Bangladesh is in the early stages of developing AI policy. The government's Smart Bangladesh Vision 2041 includes digital transformation and AI integration goals, and the ICT Division has begun discussions about governance frameworks. However, comprehensive AI regulation comparable to the EU AI Act doesn't yet exist here. It's an active area where academic institutions, government, and industry are beginning to engage — and one where students like us have a real role to play in shaping the conversation."**

---

**Q: What is the most dangerous application of AI?**

*Suggested speaker: Leader or Member 6*

**"That's a nuanced question with near-term and long-term dimensions. In the near term, many experts point to AI-generated misinformation — deepfakes and synthetic media that can manipulate public opinion at scale. We saw a 10x increase in deepfake fraud in 2023 alone. In the longer term, autonomous weapons systems — AI that can make lethal decisions without human oversight — raise profound ethical and legal questions with no consensus yet. Both are areas where international governance is urgently needed."**

---

## DELIVERY NOTES — LEADER SPECIFIC

**Opening:**
- Walk in, don't shuffle — project authority before you say a word
- Make eye contact with 3–4 different people before speaking
- The x86 name explanation is a story — tell it like one, not like a definition
- The phone-check question is your hook — actually pause and look at the room

**Conclusion:**
- This is not a summary recital — it's a landing
- Speak the final "most consequential technology" line slowly enough that it lands
- The room should feel a moment of gravity before you thank them

**Q&A:**
- You are the traffic controller — don't let one member monopolize
- If you don't know: "That's an excellent question — let me think about that carefully. What we can say with confidence is..." then pivot to what you do know
- Keep energy up — the presentation is not over until you leave the room
