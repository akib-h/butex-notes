`[SLIDE 6 — Types of AI]`

*Step forward. Bring energy — you're about to make something technical accessible.*

**"Thank you, [Member 2's Name]."**

*Brief pause.*

**"Before we get into how AI learns, it helps to know what kind of AI we're even talking about."**

**"Researchers classify AI into three broad categories."**

*Hold up three fingers as you count them.*

**"First: Narrow AI — also called Weak AI. This is the only type of AI that actually exists today. It's designed to do one specific task, and it does it extremely well. Siri, Google Translate, Netflix recommendations, ChatGPT — all Narrow AI."**

**"Second: General AI — or AGI, Artificial General Intelligence. This hypothetical system would match a human's ability to reason, learn, and apply knowledge across any domain. We do not have this yet."**

**"Third: Superintelligence — an AI that surpasses all of human intellectual capability combined. This is firmly in the realm of theory and speculation — but it's the concept that keeps AI researchers up at night."**

*Pause. Speak pointedly.*

**"So everything you've seen in the news — everything we'll show you today — is Narrow AI. And yet, it's already transforming the world."**

---

`[SLIDE 7 — Machine Learning Explained]`

**"So how does Narrow AI get so good at its specific task? The answer is Machine Learning."**

**"Machine Learning is a subset of AI where systems learn from data rather than being explicitly programmed with rules."**

**"Here's an analogy: Imagine teaching a child what a cat looks like. You don't give them a rulebook — 'four legs, pointed ears, fur.' You just show them thousands of pictures of cats and say 'cat' each time. Eventually, they recognize cats on their own — even ones they've never seen before."**

**"Machine learning works the same way — but with data instead of picture books."**

*Pause. Then count the three types on your fingers.*

**"There are three main types of machine learning."**

**"Supervised Learning — the model is trained on labeled data. Think of a spam filter trained on thousands of emails labeled 'spam' or 'not spam' — it learns the patterns and starts classifying new emails correctly."**

**"Unsupervised Learning — the model finds patterns in unlabeled data on its own. Used when you want to discover structure you didn't know was there — like grouping customers by purchasing behavior without telling the system what the groups should be."**

**"Reinforcement Learning — the model learns by trial and error, receiving rewards for good actions. This is exactly how you learned to play a video game: try, fail, get a score, improve. Except the AI plays millions of games per hour."**

**"This type of learning is how AI mastered Chess, Go, and even how robots learn to walk."**

---

`[SLIDE 8 — Deep Learning Explained]`
`[SLIDE 9 — Neural Network Diagram]`

*Step slightly to the side and gesture toward the slide diagram.*

**"Now — what's Deep Learning, and how is it different from regular Machine Learning?"**

**"Deep Learning is a specialized subset of machine learning that uses Artificial Neural Networks — computational systems loosely inspired by the human brain."**

*Point to diagram on slide.*

**"Look at this diagram. A neural network consists of layers: an input layer, multiple hidden layers, and an output layer. Each layer contains nodes — like neurons — that process and pass information forward."**

**"The 'deep' in Deep Learning refers to the depth of these layers — modern neural networks can have hundreds of layers, which allows them to learn incredibly complex patterns."**

*Pause for one beat.*

**"Here's a good way to think about it: imagine a factory with many assembly stations. Raw material — let's say an image — enters at one end. The first station detects edges and basic shapes. The next detects textures. The next detects parts. By the end of the line, the factory has identified: this is a human face."**

**"That's what happens inside a deep neural network — layer by layer, raw input becomes meaningful output."**

**"Deep learning is what powers face recognition on your phone, real-time language translation, and the AI image generators you've seen online."**

**"It requires enormous amounts of data and computing power — but when those conditions are met, it performs like nothing else in the history of computing."**

*Pause. Nod toward Member 4.*

**"So we now understand what AI is and how it learns. The next natural question is: where is it being used? [Member 4's Name] will walk us through the real world."**

## BACKUP ANALOGY — REINFORCEMENT LEARNING

> Use if the audience looks confused or during Q&A:

**"The simplest way to understand reinforcement learning is AlphaGo. No one programmed it with chess-like rules. Instead, the AI played millions of games against itself, rewarded for every victory. Over time, it developed strategies that no human had ever conceived — and defeated the world Go champion in 2016. That's reinforcement learning: reward good outcomes, penalize bad ones, let the system discover its own path."**
