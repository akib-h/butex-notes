## 📖 Overview

Flax (*Linum usitatissimum* L.) is one of the oldest known bast fibres in the world, with a textile history spanning over **30,000 years**. The fibre extracted from the flax plant's stem is known commercially as **linen** — a word synonymous with fine quality, durability, and natural luxury. It is classified as a **natural, cellulosic, bast, multi-cellular** fibre.

Flax holds a unique position in the world of technical textiles, being both one of the first plant fibres ever used by humans and simultaneously one of the most researched for modern applications — from automotive composites to medical fabrics.
