## 3.1 Definition of Grading

By the term **grading of jute**, we mean the **assortment of raw jute according to their quality**. Jute fibres are graded in three ways:

---

## 3.2 Three Bases of Grading

### A. On the Basis of Fibre Properties

Fibre characteristics that influence grading (from higher to lower standard):

1. **Length**
2. **Strength**
3. **Fineness**
4. **Colour**
5. **Lustre**
6. **Roots and Cleanliness**
7. **Uniformity in Colour**

### B. On the Basis of Export

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| A Bottom | Highest export quality |
| B Bottom | Second quality |
| C Bottom | Third quality |
| X Bottom | Lowest export quality |

### C. On the Basis of Quality of Raw Jute (in Practice)

| Grading Type | Raw Jute Condition |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **Pucca Grading** | Raw jute from which roots **have been cut** |
| **Kutcha Grading** | Raw jute from which roots **have NOT been cut** |

## 3.3 BD Grading System — Flow Chart

```
                    BD Grading System
                          |
          ________________|________________
         |                                 |
   Kutcha Grading                   Pucca Grading
         |                          _______|_______
    _____|_____                    |               |
   |     |     |              White Jute      Tossa Jute
  Top  Middle Bottom          (BW)             (BT)
         |     |
      B-Bottom  |          → BW Special       → BT Special
      C-Bottom  |          → BW-A             → BT-A
      X-Bottom  |          → BW-B             → BT-B
      Habijabi  |          → BW-C             → BT-C
                           → BW-D             → BT-D
                           → BW-E             → BT-E

BW = Bangla White
BT = Bangla Tossa
```

## 3.4 Pucca Grading

Grading is done **after the barky ends are cut off**. There are **six grades** for both White Jute and Tossa Jute.

### 3.4.1 White Jute (Bangla White — BW) Grades

| Grade | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| **BW Special (BWS)** | White/creamy white. Finest texture, very strong and very good lustre. Completely free from any defect, clean cut, well hackled, and entirely free from red ends. |
| **BW-A (BWA)** | White to light cream. Fine texture, strong and very good lustre. Completely free from any blemish. Clean cut, well hackled, entirely free from red ends. |
| **BW-B (BWB)** | Light cream to straw colour. Good texture, strong and good lustre. Free from blemish. Clean cut and well hackled, excluding red ends. |
| **BW-C (BWC)** | Light grey/light reddish to straw colour. Clean fibre of sound strength and average lustre. Free from hard specks, croppy/hard gummy tops. Well-cut, well hackled, free from black roots; red soft ends permissible. |
| **BW-D (BWD)** | Any colour, average strength, occasional bark and specks. Slightly croppy and gummy tops permissible. Well-cut and well hackled; red ends also permissible. |
| **BW-E (BWE)** | Any colour, any strength but free from perished fibres. Free from unretted jute and sticks, but bark and hard centre permissible. Rough cut and well hackled. |

### 3.4.2 Tossa Jute (Bangla Tossa — BT) Grades

| Grade | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| **BT Special (BTS)** | Uniform golden or reddish colour. Finest texture, very strong, very good lustre, clean cut and well hackled. Completely free from any defects. |
| **BT-A (BTA)** | (a) Uniform silver grey to golden; OR (b) Uniform light golden to reddish. Fine texture, strong and good lustre. Clean cut and well hackled. Completely free from any blemish. |
| **BT-B (BTB)** | (a) Light to medium grey; OR (b) Light grey or reddish (excluding dark gray). Sound clean, good texture, average lustre, clean cut and well hackled. Free from any defects. |
| **BT-C (BTC)** | Mixed colours, average strength, occasional bark and soft specks allowable but free from runners. Slightly croppy and gummy tops permissible. Well cut and hackled. Free from black root ends. |
| **BT-D (BTD)** | Mixed colours, average strength, occasional bark and specks allowable but free from runners. Croppy and gummy tops permissible. Rough cut and hackled. Free from black root ends. |
| **BT-E (BTE)** | Any colour, any strength but free from perished fibres. Free from unretted jute and stick; bark and hard centre permissible. Rough cut and hackled. |

### Summary by Grade Level

| Grade Level | BW & BT Special | BW-A & BT-A | BW-B & BT-B | BW-C & BT-C | BW-D & BT-D | BW-E & BT-E |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Texture** | Finest | Fine | Good | Average | Any | Any |
| **Strength** | Very strong | Strong | Strong | Sound | Average | Any |
| **Lustre** | Very good | Very good | Good | Average | - | - |
| **Defects** | None | None | None | Minor | Some | Any (except perished) |

## 3.5 Kutcha Grading

Grading is done on raw jute from which roots have **NOT** been cut. Kutcha bales are graded as follows:

| Grade | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| **Tops** | Very strong fiber, excellent colour and luster, free from all defects. Cutting: not more than White 15%, Tossa 10%. |
| **Middles** | Strong, sound fibres, average colour and luster, free from all specks, runners and harsh crop end. Cutting: White 25%, Tossa 15%. |
| **Bottoms** | Sound fibres, medium strength, free from all hard-centred jute. Cutting: White 30%, Tossa 20%. |
| **B-Bottoms** | Sound fibre, medium strength, not suitable for higher grade. Cutting: White 35%, Tossa 25%. |
| **C-Bottoms** | Medium strength fibre, any colour but free from runners and croppiness. |
| **X-Bottoms (Cross Bottoms)** | Weak, harsh jute but free from tangled jute and stick; jute of any sort. |
| **Habijabi** | Tangled, ravelled jute of any sort, free from dust and cuttings. |

---

## 3.6 Factors Affecting Gradation of Jute

1. **Nature of soil, water, rainfall, humidity, and topography**
2. **Seasonal variation** — quality varies from season to season and place to place
3. **Different cultivation practices** followed while growing jute
4. **Methods used in retting, extraction, washing, drying, and storage**

## 3.7 Practical Example — Grading Calculation

> **Problem:** A batch of jute fibre has the following properties:
> - Colour: Light cream to straw
> - Texture: Good
> - Strength: Strong
> - Lustre: Good
> - Free from blemish, clean cut, well hackled, excluding red ends
>
> **Question:** What Pucca grade would this fibre receive?

**Solution:**

Compare against Pucca White Jute grades:
- BWS: Requires "white/creamy" colour — **does not match** (ours is "light cream to straw")
- BWA: Requires "white to light cream" — **partially matches** colour
- **BWB**: Requires "light cream to straw colour, good texture, strong, good lustre, free from blemish, clean cut and well hackled, excluding red ends" — **MATCHES**

> **Answer: BW-B (Bangla White-B)**
