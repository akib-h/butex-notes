## 8.2 Identification Test of Jute Fibre

### 8.2.1 Non-Technical Tests

#### i) Feeling Test

- Jute feel: **Cool to touch, feels smooth and leathery** (some sources say: stiff and harsh hand)
- Different fibres show different behaviour when placed on the hand
- Jute is **stiff and harsh to human skin** — feels bad against skin
- Cotton feels soft and warm

#### ii) Burning Test

| Property | Jute (Cellulosic) | Cotton (Cellulosic) |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Burning behaviour** | Burns easily, does NOT melt | Burns easily, does not melt |
| **Flame** | Yellow flame with little ash | Yellow flame |
| **Smell** | Similar to **burned paper** | Similar to burned paper |
| **Afterglow** | Soldering red after glow | No afterglow |
| **Ash** | Light grey/white ash | Light grey ash |

> Both jute and cotton are cellulosic — they burn with a yellow flame and smell similar to burning paper.

### 8.2.2 Technical Tests

#### i) Microscopic Test

- **Cross-section:** Polygonal shaped cells with **many ultimate cells** visible (5–15) in cross-section
- **Longitudinal view:** Multiple ultimate cells; irregular lumen visible along length
- These features easily identify jute fibre

#### ii) Solubility / Chemical Test

- Jute fibre is **easily dissolved in 58% H₂SO₄** in warm condition
- Cotton also dissolves in concentrated H₂SO₄

#### iii) Density Measurement Test

```
Specific gravity of jute = 1.48–1.5

To identify by density:
- Prepare liquids of known density
- Float the fibre sample
- If fibre sinks in liquid of SG = 1.45 but floats in SG = 1.55 → SG of fibre ≈ 1.5 → likely jute
```

#### iv) Staining Test

- Different fibres stain differently with dyes
- Jute shows strong affinity for **basic (cationic) dyes**
- This can help distinguish from cotton (which requires direct/reactive dyes for good take-up)

## 8.3 Methods of Upgrading Jute Fibre

To overcome jute's disadvantages (brittleness, roughness, etc.), several upgrading methods are used:

1. **Use of improved type of chemical emulsion** (batching oil)
2. **Use of improved type of emulsion mixing apparatus**
3. **By using staple mixing** methods
4. **Improved type of softening / speading machine (m/c)**
5. **By enzymatic methods** — enzymatic treatment to remove lignin and hemicellulose, improving softness

## 8.4 Jute Diversification — Detailed Discussion

Jute has entered various diversified sectors where natural fibres are gradually becoming better substitutes for synthetic ones:

### Industries Using Diversified Jute

1. **Paper industry** — jute pulp paper
2. **Celluloid products** — films
3. **Non-woven textiles**
4. **Composites and geotextiles** — most popular in agricultural sector
5. **Home textiles** — floor coverings (woven/tufted/piled carpets), UV protection, sound/heat insulation

### Properties that Make Jute Suitable for Home Textiles

- **Strong, durable, colour-and-light-fast fibre**
- UV protection, sound and heat insulation
- **Low thermal conduction** and **anti-static properties** — wise choice for home décor
- Fabrics made from jute fibres are **carbon-dioxide neutral** and **naturally decomposable**

### Geotextile Application

- Geotextile is a lightly woven fabric made from natural fibres used for:
  - **Soil erosion control**
  - **Seed protection**
  - **Weed control**
  - Various agricultural and landscaping uses
- The bio-degradable jute geotextile left to rot on the ground:
  - **Keeps the ground cool**
  - **Makes the land more fertile**
  - Can be used for more than **one year**

## 8.5 Why Jute Cannot be Bleached to Pure White

```
Reason:
1. Jute contains 10.8–13% lignin
2. Lignin is an aromatic phenolic polymer
3. Lignin absorbs visible and UV light → causes yellowing
4. Bleaching agents attack lignin but also damage cellulose
5. Result: Attempting to fully remove lignin (to get white) also destroys the fibre strength
6. Therefore: Jute can be bleached partially but NEVER to pure white

Alternative: Treatment with strong NaOH converts jute to a wool-like fibre
```

## 8.6 Environmental Advantages vs Synthetic Fibres

| Aspect | Jute | Synthetic (Polypropylene/Plastic) |
|--------|------|----------------------------------|
| Biodegradability | **100% — decomposes in 1–2 years** | Hundreds of years |
| Toxic residue | **None** | Releases toxic substances |
| CO₂ emission | **Carbon neutral** (absorbs during growth) | High carbon footprint |
| Renewable | **Yes — annual crop** | No (petroleum-based) |
| Soil pollution | **No** | Yes (micro-plastics) |
| Water pollution | **No** | Yes (micro-plastics) |
