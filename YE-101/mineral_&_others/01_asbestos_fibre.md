# Asbestos Fibre  
**Date:** April 5, 2026  

## 1. Definition
- Asbestos is a group of naturally occurring **silicate mineral fibres**.
- Types: Chrysotile (white), Amosite (brown), Crocidolite (blue), Tremolite, Actinolite, Anthophyllite.

## 2. Properties
1. **Heat resistance** – withstands >1000°C.  
2. **High tensile strength** – durable under stress.  
3. **Chemical resistance** – inert to most acids/alkalis.  

![Asbestos fibre micrograph](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0e/Asbestos_fibres.jpg)

## 3. Health Hazards
- **Asbestosis**: chronic lung disease due to fibre inhalation.  
- **Mesothelioma**: rare cancer linked to asbestos exposure.  
- WHO estimates **200,000+ deaths annually** from asbestos-related diseases.  

## 4. Industrial Uses
- Historically used in **construction, insulation, brake linings, roofing sheets**.  
- Now banned in >50 countries due to health risks.  

## 5. Example
- **Asbestos cement sheets** widely used in South Asia for roofing until bans.
