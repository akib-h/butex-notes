Internal Architecture
Structural Layers — Cotton vs Wool vs Silk

These three fibres have the most complex internal layer structures and are most commonly compared in exams.

🌿 Cotton — Layer Structure (Outer → Inner)
Cuticle Wax + pectin outer coat; water-repellent until scoured
Primary Wall Thin cellulose microfibrils; random orientation
S1 layer Outer secondary wall; fibrillar angle ~70°
S2 layer Middle (thickest) secondary wall; ~20–30° angle; gives strength
S3 layer Inner secondary wall; thin; ~0–45° angle
Lumen Central canal; collapses on drying → kidney shape
🐑 Wool — Layer Structure (Outer → Inner)
Cuticle (scales) Overlapping keratinous scales; serrated edge; causes felting
Epicuticle Outermost membrane; hydrophobic (resists water initially)
Ortho-cortex Left side; more water-absorbent; swells more → causes natural crimp
Para-cortex Right side; less absorbent; denser; bilateral arrangement → crimp
Medulla Central air-filled canal; only in coarse wool; absent in fine Merino
🦋 Silk — Layer Structure
Sericin Outer gum (25–30% of raw silk); holds two fibroin strands together; removed by degumming
Fibroin core Two triangular protein filaments; gives strength and lustre; ~70–75% of silk
β-sheet structure Crystalline regions in fibroin; antiparallel chains; responsible for tensile strength
Note: Silk has NO lumen. It is a solid continuous protein filament — the only natural continuous-filament fibre.
🌾 Jute — Layer Structure
Middle Lamella Pectin + lignin cement; binds adjacent cells into bundles
Primary Wall Thin cellulose + lignin + hemicellulose
Secondary Wall Thick; cellulose microfibrils embedded in lignin matrix; gives rigidity
Lumen Large, round, open; clearly visible under low magnification
Lignin content (15–25%) is what makes jute stiff, golden-coloured, and difficult to bleach/dye evenly.

Key Morphological Identifiers

These are the single most distinguishing features examiners test.

Fibre The ONE Feature That Identifies It Exam Trick / Common Q
CottonTwisted ribbon with convolutions (longitudinal)"Why is the cross-section kidney-shaped?" → lumen collapses on drying
JutePolygonal cells with large round lumen + lignin"Why can't jute be bleached white?" → lignin absorbs chemicals
FlaxNodes (bamboo joints) on longitudinal surface"How to distinguish flax from hemp?" → flax nodes more regular; smaller, thinner
WoolOverlapping scales on longitudinal; bilateral cortex"Why does wool felt?" → Differential Friction Effect from scales
SilkTwo triangular fibroin filaments in cross-section"Why is silk lustrous?" → triangular prism acts as light reflector
KapokHollow tube with enormous lumen (80%+)"Why can't kapok be spun?" → too smooth + too short; no cohesion
CoirThickest cell wall of all natural fibres"Why is coir water-resistant?" → thick lignified wall repels water
RamieVery large irregular lumen; widest bast fibre cell"What is unique about ramie?" → longest + bacteriostatic + silky
