## 9. Why Wool is Easy to Dye

(QB 18 from handnotes â€” Exam question)

1. **High amorphous region:** Wool has 70â€“75% amorphous region. Dye molecules can **easily penetrate** into the amorphous regions of the fibre (crystalline regions are too compact for large dye molecules)

2. **Polar functional groups:** Wool contains amino (â€“NHâ‚‚), carboxyl (â€“COOH), and hydroxyl (â€“OH) groups in its keratin protein. These **bond well with dye molecules** (especially acid dyes and reactive dyes)

3. **Easy dye penetration:** The amorphous, open structure **allows dyes to penetrate easily** into the fibre

4. **Large surface area:** Wool's **crimp and scaly surface** provide a larger surface area for **dye uptake**

5. **Water absorption:** Wool **absorbs water, swelling the fibre** and increasing dye diffusion into the polymer system

**Dyes suitable for wool:**
- Acid dyes
- Basic (cationic) dyes
- Mordant dyes
- Pre-metallised dyes
- Reactive dyes
