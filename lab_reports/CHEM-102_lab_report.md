## Experiment 01 — Preparing 0.1M HCl Solution and Standardization

**Date:** 19 May 2022

**Essential Chemicals:** HCl, Na₂CO₃, Methyl red  
**Essential Apparatus:** Volumetric flask, Burette, Conical flask, Pipet, Funnel, Balance

### Step 1: Prepare 0.1M Na₂CO₃ Standard Solution (250 mL)

- Molecular weight of Na₂CO₃: M = 106 g
- Volume: V = 250 mL, Concentration: C = 0.1 M

$$w = \frac{C \cdot M \cdot V}{1000} = \frac{0.1 \times 106 \times 250}{1000} = 2.65 \text{ g}$$

**Actual concentration of Na₂CO₃:**

$$C_{actual} = \frac{w_{taken} \times C}{w_{required}} = \frac{2.65 \times 0.1}{2.65} = 0.098 \text{ M}$$

### Step 2: Prepare 250 mL of 0.1M HCl

- Commercial HCl concentration: S₁ = 11.3 M
- Using $S_1V_1 = S_2V_2$:

$$V_1 = \frac{S_2 \times V_2}{S_1} = \frac{0.1 \times 250}{11.3} = 2.21 \text{ mL}$$

Take 2.21 mL of conc. HCl and dilute to 250 mL.

### Step 3: Determine HCl Concentration by Standardization

**Reaction:** Na₂CO₃ + 2HCl → 2NaCl + CO₂ + H₂O

**Observation Table:**

| SL | Vol. Na₂CO₃ (mL) | Vol. H₂O + Indicator | 1st Reading | 2nd Reading | Diff. | Average (mL) | Conc. HCl (M) |
|:--:|:-----------------:|:---------------------:|:-----------:|:-----------:|:-----:|:------------:|:-------------:|
| 1 | 5 | 20 mL + 1 drop methyl red | 0 | 10.2 | 10.2 | — | — |
| 2 | 5 | 20 mL + 1 drop methyl red | 10.2 | 20.3 | 10.1 | 10.13 | 0.0967 M |
| 3 | 5 | 20 mL + 1 drop methyl red | 20.3 | 30.4 | 10.1 | — | — |

**Calculation:**

$$S_{HCl} = \frac{2 \times V_{Na_2CO_3} \times S_{Na_2CO_3}}{V_{HCl}} = \frac{2 \times 5 \times 0.098}{10.13} = 0.0967 \text{ M}$$

$$\% \text{ error} = \frac{0.1 - 0.0967}{0.1} \times 100 = 3.3\%$$

> **Result:** % error = **3.3%**

## Experiment 02 — Preparation of 0.1M NaOH Solution and Standardization

**Date:** 30 May 2022

**Essential Chemicals:** Solid NaOH, Oxalic acid (HOOC-COOH·2H₂O), Methyl red  
**Essential Apparatus:** Volumetric flask, Conical flask, Burette, Pipet, Funnel, Balance

### Step 1: Prepare 0.1M Oxalic Acid Standard Solution (250 mL)

- M (oxalic acid) = 126 g

$$w = \frac{0.1 \times 126 \times 250}{1000} = 3.15 \text{ g}$$

**Actual concentration** (if 3.10 g taken):

$$C_{actual} = \frac{3.10 \times 0.1}{3.15} = 0.098 \text{ M}$$

### Step 2: Prepare 0.1M NaOH Solution (250 mL)

- M (NaOH) = 40 g

$$w = \frac{0.1 \times 40 \times 250}{1000} = 1 \text{ g of NaOH}$$

### Step 3: Standardize NaOH by Standard Oxalic Acid

**Reaction:** 2NaOH + HOOC-COOH → NaOOC-COONa + 2H₂O

**Observation Table:**

| SL | Vol. Oxalic Acid (mL) | Vol. H₂O + Indicator | 1st Reading | 2nd Reading | Diff. | Average (mL) | Conc. NaOH (M) |
|:--:|:---------------------:|:--------------------:|:-----------:|:-----------:|:-----:|:------------:|:--------------:|
| 1 | 5 | 20 | 0.00 | 10.2 | 10.2 | — | — |
| 2 | 5 | 20 | 10.1 | 20.3 | 10.1 | 10.13 | 0.0967 M |
| 3 | 5 | 20 | 20.3 | 30.4 | 10.1 | — | — |

**Calculation:**

$$S_{NaOH} = \frac{2 \times V_{oxalic} \times S_{oxalic}}{V_{NaOH}} = \frac{2 \times 5 \times 0.098}{10.13} = 0.0967 \text{ M}$$

$$\% \text{ error} = \frac{0.1 - 0.0967}{0.1} \times 100 = 3.3\%$$

> **Result:** % error = **3.3%**

## Experiment 05 — Preparation of Chrome Alum [K₂SO₄·Cr₂(SO₄)₃·24H₂O]

**Date:** 2 June 2022

**Essential Chemicals:**
- K₂Cr₂O₇ = 10 g
- Conc. H₂SO₄ = 8 mL
- Absolute Alcohol (C₂H₅OH) = 10 mL
- H₂O = 40 mL

**Essential Equipment:** Beaker, Glass rod, Ice bath, Balance

### Reactions

$$K_2Cr_2O_7 + C_2H_5OH + 4H_2SO_4 \rightarrow K_2SO_4 + Cr_2(SO_4)_3 + 4H_2O + 3[O]$$

$$C_2H_5OH + [O] \rightarrow CH_3CHO + H_2O$$

$$K_2SO_4 + Cr_2(SO_4)_3 + 24H_2O \equiv K_2SO_4 \cdot Cr_2(SO_4)_3 \cdot 24H_2O \text{ (violet crystal)}$$

### Flow Chart

```
H₂O (40 mL) + H₂SO₄ (8 mL) + K₂Cr₂O₇ (10 g)
        ↓ Dissolve (warm if needed)
        ↓ Cool below room temp
        ↓ Add C₂H₅OH drop by drop, shake with glass rod
        ↓ Put mixture in ice bath, add 5 mL C₂H₅OH
        ↓ Stay overnight → violet colour crystals appear
        ↓ Filter, dry with soft tissue paper
```

### Applications of Chrome Alum

1. Used in **tanning of leather** — Cr(III) stabilizes leather by cross-linking collagen fibres (now largely obsolete; chromium(III) sulphate preferred)
2. Used in **gelatin emulsions** in photographic film as a hardener

## Experiment 06 — Preparation of Mohr's Salt [FeSO₄·(NH₄)₂SO₄·6H₂O]

**Date:** 2 June 2022

**Essential Chemicals:**
- Hydrate FeSO₄ = 12.5 g
- (NH₄)₂SO₄ = 5.5 g (note: lab notes show 3.5 g; standard ratio requires ~5.5 g)
- 2N H₂SO₄ = 5 mL
- H₂O = 18 mL

**Essential Equipment:** Beaker, Glass rod, Ice bath, Balance

### Reaction

$$FeSO_4 + (NH_4)_2SO_4 + 6H_2O \rightarrow FeSO_4 \cdot (NH_4)_2SO_4 \cdot 6H_2O \text{ (Mohr's salt)}$$

### Flow Chart

```
H₂O (6 mL) + FeSO₄ (12.5 g) + (NH₄)₂SO₄ (5.5 g) + 2N H₂SO₄ (5 mL)
        ↓ Dissolve (warm if necessary), filter below 40°C
        ↓ Put beaker in ice bath, stay overnight
        ↓ Salt crystals appear
        ↓ Filter and dry with soft tissue paper
```

**Acid correction:**  
20 mL of 2N H₂SO₄ contains $\frac{20 \times 2}{36} = 1.11$ mL of 36N H₂SO₄  
Add H₂O = (20 − 1.11) = **18.89 mL H₂O**

### Applications of Mohr's Salt

1. **Analytical chemistry** — preferred source of ferrous ions due to extended shelf life and resistance to oxidation
2. **Sulphuric acid is often added** to solutions using ferrous ammonium sulphate to reduce oxidation of ferric iron
3. **Filtration** — used to filter/filtrate impure water
