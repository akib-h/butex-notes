## Experiment 01 — Preparation of 0.1N HCl & Standardization with Na₂CO₃

**Aim:** Determine the strength of dilute HCl by titrating against standard Na₂CO₃ solution.

**Theory:**

$$Na_2CO_3 + 2HCl \rightarrow 2NaCl + CO_2 + H_2O$$

Indicator: **Methyl orange** (weak base, yellow in unionised form). End point = pinkish-red colour.  
Molarity equation: $a_1M_1V_1 = a_2M_2V_2$

**Apparatus:** Burette (50 mL), Pipette (10 mL), Conical flask (100 mL), Burette stand, Funnel, Glazed tile, Measuring flask (100 mL)  
**Chemicals:** HCl, Na₂CO₃, Methyl orange

### Procedure

**i) Preparation of 0.1N Na₂CO₃ Standard Solution (250 mL):**

- M.W. of Na₂CO₃ = 106 g; Eq. wt = 106/2 = 53 g
- 250 mL of 0.1N Na₂CO₃ contains: $\frac{53 \times 250 \times 0.1}{1000} = 1.325$ g
- Normality factor = $\frac{1.327}{1.325} = 1.0015$N
- ∴ Strength of Na₂CO₃ standard solution = 1.0015 × 0.1 = **0.10015N**

Weigh out 1.325 g Na₂CO₃, dissolve in water in 250 mL volumetric flask, add water to mark.

**ii) Preparation of 0.1N HCl from 37% and 1.18 Specific Gravity conc. HCl:**

- M.W. of HCl = 36.5 g/L; Volume = 36.5/1.18 = 30.9 mL
- 37% HCl = 30.9 mL; 100% HCl = (30.9 × 100)/37 = 83.5 mL
- 250 mL of 0.1N HCl contains: $\frac{83.5 \times 250 \times 0.1}{1000} = 2.0875$ mL

**iii) Standardize HCl by 0.1N Na₂CO₃:**

Take 10 mL Na₂CO₃ in conical flask. Add 2 drops methyl orange. Add HCl from burette with constant shaking until orange colour disappears (end point). Repeat.

### Observation Table

| No. | Vol. Na₂CO₃ V₁(mL) | Initial (mL) | Final (mL) | Diff. (mL) | Average V₂ (mL) | S₁ (N) | S₂ (N) |
|:---:|:-------------------:|:------------:|:----------:|:----------:|:---------------:|:------:|:------:|
| 1 | 10 | 0 | 9.5 | 9.5 | — | — | — |
| 2 | 10 | 9.5 | 19 | 9.5 | 9.53 | 0.10015 | 0.105 |
| 3 | 10 | 19 | 28.6 | 9.6 | — | — | — |

### Calculation

$$V_1S_1 = V_2S_2 \implies S_2 = \frac{V_1S_1}{V_2} = \frac{10 \times 0.10015}{9.53} = 0.105 \text{ N}$$

> **Result:** Strength of HCl = **0.105 N**

## Experiment 02 — Preparation of 0.1N NaOH & Standardization with Oxalic Acid

**Aim:** Determine the strength of NaOH by titrating against standard oxalic acid.

**Theory:**

$$2(COOH) + 2NaOH \rightarrow 2(COONa) + 2H_2O$$

Indicator: **Phenolphthalein** (end point = permanent pink). pH at end point > 7.0 (alkaline).

**Apparatus:** Burette (50 mL), Pipette (10 mL), Conical flask (100 mL), Burette stand, Funnel, Glazed tile  
**Chemicals:** NaOH, Oxalic acid, Phenolphthalein

### Procedure

**i) 0.1N Standard Oxalic Acid (250 mL):**

- M.W. of H₂C₂O₄·2H₂O = 126 g; Eq. wt = 126/2 = 63 g
- 250 mL 0.1N contains: $\frac{63 \times 0.1 \times 250}{1000} = 1.575$ g
- Normality factor = 1.578/1.575 = 1.0019
- ∴ Strength = 1.0019 × 0.1 = **0.10019N**

**ii) 0.1N NaOH (250 mL):**

- M.W. = Eq. wt = 40 g
- 250 mL 0.1N contains: $\frac{40 \times 0.1 \times 250}{1000} = 1$ g NaOH

**iii) Standardize NaOH by 0.1N Oxalic Acid:**

Take 10 mL oxalic acid in conical flask, add 2–3 drops phenolphthalein. Titrate with NaOH until permanent pink. Repeat.

### Observation Table

| No. | Vol. H₂C₂O₄·2H₂O V₁(mL) | Initial (mL) | Final (mL) | Diff. (mL) | Average V₂ (mL) | S₁ (N) | S₂ (N) |
|:---:|:-------------------------:|:------------:|:----------:|:----------:|:---------------:|:------:|:------:|
| 1 | 10 | 0 | 9.8 | 9.8 | — | — | — |
| 2 | 10 | 9.8 | 19.5 | 9.7 | 9.766 | 0.10019 | 0.102 |
| 3 | 10 | 19.5 | 29.3 | 9.8 | — | — | — |

### Calculation

$$S_2 = \frac{V_1 S_1}{V_2} = \frac{10 \times 0.10019}{9.766} = 0.102 \text{ N}$$

> **Result:** Strength of NaOH = **0.102 N**

## Experiment 03 — Preparation of 0.1N H₂SO₄ & Standardization with Na₂CO₃

**Aim:** Determine the strength of dilute H₂SO₄ by titrating against standard Na₂CO₃.

**Theory:**

$$Na_2CO_3 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + H_2O + CO_2$$

H₂SO₄ is a diprotic acid; 1N solution contains 49.04 g H₂SO₄. Indicator: **Methyl orange**. End point = red colour.

**Apparatus:** Volumetric flask, Burette, Burette stand, Pipette, Conical flask, Digital balance, Heating mantle  
**Chemicals:** H₂SO₄, Na₂CO₃, Methyl orange

### Procedure

**i) 0.1N Na₂CO₃ (250 mL):** (Same as Exp. 01) → Strength = **0.10015N**

**ii) Preparation of 0.1N H₂SO₄ from 98% and 1.84 Specific Gravity:**

- M.W. = 98.08 g/mol; Eq. wt = 49.04 g/mol
- Volume = 49.04/1.84 = 26.652 mL
- 98% H₂SO₄ = 26.652 mL; 100% = (26.652 × 100)/98 = 27.196 mL
- 250 mL 0.1N H₂SO₄ contains: $\frac{27.196 \times 0.1 \times 250}{1000} = 0.6799$ mL

**iii) Standardize H₂SO₄ by 0.1N Na₂CO₃:**

Take 10 mL 0.1N Na₂CO₃ in conical flask. Add 2–3 drops methyl orange. Titrate with H₂SO₄ until red. Repeat.

## Experiment 05 — Analysis of Unknown Salt (Acidic & Basic Radical) — Salt I: NH₄Cl

### Preliminary Investigation

| Property | Observation | Inference |
|:---------|:-----------|:---------|
| Physical state | Solid | — |
| Colour | White | Cu²⁺, Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺, Ni²⁺, Mn²⁺, Co²⁺ absent |
| Odour | Ammonium smell | NH₄⁺ may be present |
| Solubility | Soluble in water | — |
| Flame test | No characteristic flame | Cu²⁺, Ca²⁺, Ba²⁺, Sn²⁺, Pb²⁺, Zn²⁺ absent |

### A. Identification of Acidic Radical (Cl⁻)

**a) Preliminary Test:**

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + dil. H₂SO₄ | No gas evolved | Group A anion (CO₃²⁻, CH₃COO⁻, NO₂⁻, S²⁻, SO₃²⁻ absent) |
| 2 | Salt + conc. H₂SO₄ + heat + NH₄OH rod | Colourless gas, pungent, white dense fumes of NH₄Cl | Group B anion (Cl⁻ may be) |

**b) Confirmative Test:**

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + AgNO₃; dissolve ppt in NH₄OH | Curdy white ppt; ppt soluble in NH₄OH | **Cl⁻ confirmed** |
| 2 | Chromyl chloride test: Salt + K₂Cr₂O₇(1:2) + conc. H₂SO₄ + heat; pass vapour through NaOH; add CH₃COOH + Pb(CH₃COO)₂ | Reddish-orange gas; solution becomes yellow; yellow ppt of lead chromate | **Cl⁻ confirmed** |

**Chemical Reactions:**
$$2NH_4Cl + H_2SO_4 \xrightarrow{\Delta} (NH_4)_2SO_4 + 2HCl\uparrow$$
$$NH_4OH + HCl \rightarrow NH_4Cl + H_2O \text{ (white dense fumes)}$$
$$NH_4Cl + 2AgNO_3 \rightarrow 2AgCl\downarrow + NH_4NO_3 \text{ (curdy white ppt)}$$
$$AgCl + NH_4OH \rightarrow [Ag(NH_3)_2]Cl + 2H_2O \text{ (diammine silver(I) chloride, soluble)}$$

### B. Identification of Basic Radical (NH₄⁺)

**a) Preliminary Test:**

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + NaOH + heat; red litmus at mouth | Smell of NH₃; litmus turns blue | Zero Group (NH₄⁺ may be) |

**b) Confirmative Test:**

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + NaOH + Nessler's reagent | Reddish-brown ppt | **NH₄⁺ confirmed** |
| 2 | Salt + NaOH + heat; glass rod dipped in dil. HCl | Smell of NH₃; white dense fumes of NH₄Cl | **NH₄⁺ confirmed** |

$$NH_4Cl + NaOH \xrightarrow{\Delta} NaCl + H_2O + NH_3\uparrow$$
$$NH_3 + \text{red litmus} \rightarrow \text{litmus turns blue}$$

> **Result:** Acidic Radical = **Cl⁻**, Basic Radical = **NH₄⁺** → Salt is **NH₄Cl**

## Experiment 06 — Analysis of Unknown Salt — Salt II: CuSO₄

### Preliminary Investigation

| Solubility | Soluble in water | — |

### A. Acidic Radical (SO₄²⁻)

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + dil. H₂SO₄ | No gas evolved | Group A absent |
| 2 | Salt + conc. H₂SO₄ + heat | No gas evolved | Group B absent |
| 3 | Salt + BaCl₂ | White ppt formed | Group C (SO₄²⁻ may be) |

**Confirmative:** BaCl₂ test → White ppt insoluble in dil. HCl → **SO₄²⁻ confirmed**

$$CuSO_4 + BaCl_2 \rightarrow BaSO_4\downarrow + CuCl_2 \text{ (white ppt)}$$

### B. Basic Radical (Cu²⁺)

| SL | Experiment | Observation | Inference |
|:--:|:----------|:-----------|:---------|
| 1 | Salt + NaOH + heat | No smell of NH₃ | Zero group (NH₄⁺ absent) |
| 2 | Salt + dil. HCl | No white ppt | Group I (Pb²⁺ absent) |
| 3 | Above solution + H₂S gas | Black ppt | Group II (Cu²⁺, Pb²⁺ may be present) |
| 4 | Dissolve ppt in HNO₃ | Solution turns bluish-green | — |
| 5 | Part 1 + NH₄OH | Deep blue colour | **Cu²⁺ confirmed** |
| 6 | Part 2 + K₄[Fe(CN)₆] | Chocolate-brown ppt of copper ferrocyanide | **Cu²⁺ confirmed** |

$$Cu^{2+} + H_2S \rightarrow CuS\downarrow + 2H^+ \text{ (black ppt)}$$
$$Cu(NO_3)_2 + 4NH_4OH \rightarrow [Cu(NH_3)_4](NO_3)_2 + 4H_2O \text{ (deep blue)}$$
$$2Cu(NO_3)_2 + K_4[Fe(CN)_6] \rightarrow Cu_2[Fe(CN)_6]\downarrow + 4KNO_3 \text{ (chocolate brown ppt)}$$

> **Result:** Acidic Radical = **SO₄²⁻**, Basic Radical = **Cu²⁺** → Salt is **CuSO₄**
