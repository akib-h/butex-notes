# Study of Steam Generators (Boilers)

## 1. Objectives
To study the working of various types of steam generators (steam boilers).

## 2. Introduction & Working Principle
A steam boiler is a closed pressure vessel that generates steam by transferring
heat produced by burning fuel to water.
* Fuel (usually coal) is burned in a furnace to heat water in the boiler shell.
* Hot gases from the furnace touch the surface of the boiler shell, generating steam.
* The generated steam drives a turbine for electricity generation or other
  industrial purposes.

## 3. Textile Industry Application
Fire tube boilers are most commonly used in small and medium-scale textile plants.
* **Why?** They provide the ideal steam pressure and temperature required for
  critical textile processes like dyeing, printing, drying, and finishing.
* They have lower installation and maintenance costs and are easier to manage.

## 4. Key Formula: Draught Chimney Height
$$
h = 353H \left( \frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{m+1}{mT_2} \right)
$$

*Where:*
* $h$ = draught (mm of water)
* $H$ = minimum chimney height (m)
* $T_1$ = outside air temperature (K)
* $T_2$ = mean chimney gas temperature (K)
* $m$ = mass of air per kg of fuel
