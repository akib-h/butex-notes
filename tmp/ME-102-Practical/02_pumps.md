# Study of Centrifugal & Reciprocating Pumps

## 1. Objectives
Study the working principles of Centrifugal and Reciprocating pumps.
Pumps are vital components used to raise water levels and boost water pressure.

## 2. Centrifugal Pump
* **Principle:** Water is drawn into the pump from a source. The rotating
  impeller spins the liquid outwards, providing kinetic energy.
* The pump volute (casing) catches the liquid, slowing it down.
* According to Bernoulli's principle, this deceleration converts kinetic
  energy into pressure.

## 3. Reciprocating Pump
* **Principle:** A power source uses a crank and connecting rod to translate
  rotary motion into the reciprocating (back-and-forth) motion of a piston
  inside a cylinder.
* Moving the piston outwards creates a vacuum, opening the suction valve
  to draw water in.
* Moving the piston inwards forces the water out through the delivery valve.
