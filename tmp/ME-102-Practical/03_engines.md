# Study of Engines (Petrol, Diesel, Gas)

## 1. Objectives
Familiarization with the working principles of power generators, engines,
and turbines.

## 2. Introduction
A heat engine converts heat energy into mechanical energy. Combustion of fuel
generates heat, which expands a working substance to produce useful work.
* **Internal Combustion (IC):** Combustion occurs inside the engine cylinder
  (e.g., Petrol/Diesel engines).
* **External Combustion (EC):** Combustion occurs outside the engine
  (e.g., Steam turbines).

## 3. Spark Ignition (SI) vs. Compression Ignition (CI)
| Feature | Spark Ignition (Petrol) | Compression Ignition (Diesel) |
| :------ | :---------------------- | :---------------------------- |
| **Cycle** | Otto Cycle | Diesel Cycle |
| **Fuel** | Gasoline / High octane | Diesel / High cetane |
| **Ignition** | Spark plug | High compression heat |
| **Intake** | Air-fuel mixture | Air only (fuel injected later) |

## 4. Key Formula
* **Otto Cycle Efficiency:**
$$
\eta = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma - 1}}
$$

*Where:* $r$ = compression ratio, $\gamma$ = specific heat ratio ($C_p / C_v$).
