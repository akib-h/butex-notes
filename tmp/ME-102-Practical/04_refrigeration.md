# Study of Refrigeration Unit

## 1. Objectives
Familiarization with the working principle of a refrigeration unit.

## 2. Introduction
Refrigeration is the process of removing heat from a substance under controlled
conditions, maintaining the body below the general temperature of its surroundings.

## 3. Vapour Compression Refrigeration Cycle
This system acts as a latent heat pump using alternate condensation and evaporation:
1. **Isentropic Compression:** Refrigerant vapour is compressed, increasing
   pressure and temperature.
2. **Constant Pressure Condensation:** Heat is rejected to the environment,
   turning high-pressure vapour into liquid.
3. **Throttling (Expansion):** Pressure drops sharply through an expansion valve.
4. **Constant Pressure Evaporation:** Liquid refrigerant absorbs latent heat
   from the refrigerated space and turns back into vapour.

## 4. Key Formula
* **Coefficient of Performance (COP):**
$$
\text{COP} = \frac{\text{Refrigerating Effect}}{\text{Work Input}} = \frac{T_L}{T_H - T_L}
$$

*Where:* $T_L$ = temperature of cold reservoir (K), $T_H$ = temperature of
hot reservoir (K). Valid for ideal (Carnot) cycle only.
