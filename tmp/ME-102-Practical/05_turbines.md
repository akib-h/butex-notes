# Study of Steam and Gas Turbines

## 1. Objectives
Familiarization with the working principles of Steam Turbines and Gas Turbines.

## 2. Steam Turbine (Rankine Cycle)
A mechanical device that extracts thermal energy from pressurized steam to
create rotary motion.

**Process:**
Water pumped to high pressure $\rightarrow$ Heated in boiler to create
high-pressure steam $\rightarrow$ Expanded through turbine to generate
mechanical power $\rightarrow$ Condensed back to liquid $\rightarrow$ Repeated.

## 3. Gas Turbine (Brayton Cycle)
An internal combustion engine that uses air as the working fluid.

**Process:**
Air drawn in and compressed $\rightarrow$ Fuel mixed and burned at constant
pressure $\rightarrow$ Hot gases drive the turbine $\rightarrow$ Exhaust.

## 4. Steam vs. Gas Turbine
| Feature | Gas Turbine | Steam Turbine |
| :-------- | :-------------------- | :------------------------ |
| **Fluid** | Hot gases (Air+Fuel) | Steam (Water vapour) |
| **Startup** | Faster | Slower (boiler warmup needed) |
| **Weight** | Compact and lighter | Larger and heavier |
| **Application** | Aircraft, power generation | Power plants, marine propulsion |
